# QUALITY CONTROL AND TESTING PROCEDURES
## Document ID: QC-PROC-2024-08

### 1. CONCRETE QUALITY CONTROL

**Mix Design Requirements:**
- All concrete mixes designed by certified concrete technologist
- Trial mixes required 28 days prior to production use
- Slump testing: 4-inch maximum for pumped concrete, 3-inch for conventional placement
- Air content: 6% ± 1% for exterior exposure, 4% ± 1% for interior work

**Field Testing Procedures:**
- Cylinder samples taken at frequency of 1 set per 100 cubic yards or per day, whichever is more frequent
- Slump tests performed on first load and every 4 hours thereafter
- Temperature monitoring: concrete delivery temperature between 50°F and 90°F
- Chloride ion testing for post-tensioned concrete structures

**Strength Requirements:**
- 7-day compressive strength minimum 65% of specified 28-day strength
- 28-day compressive strength must meet or exceed design specifications
- If test results fall below 85% of specified strength, investigation procedures initiated
- Core testing required if cylinder tests indicate deficiencies

### 2. STRUCTURAL STEEL INSPECTION

**Material Certification:**
- Mill test certificates required for all structural steel
- Chemical analysis and mechanical properties verification
- Impact testing for materials exposed to temperatures below 32°F
- Heat numbers tracked and correlated with placement locations

**Welding Quality Control:**
- Welder qualification per AWS D1.1 requirements
- Visual inspection of 100% of structural welds
- Ultrasonic testing (UT) of 10% of complete joint penetration welds
- Magnetic particle testing (MT) for fillet welds in critical connections

**Dimensional Tolerances:**
- Column plumbness: 1:500 maximum deviation
- Beam camber: +1/2 inch, -0 inches from specified camber
- Connection bolt hole alignment: ±1/16 inch maximum offset
- Member length tolerances per AISC Code of Standard Practice

### 3. MASONRY CONSTRUCTION QUALITY

**Material Requirements:**
- Concrete masonry units (CMU): ASTM C90 compliance
- Mortar testing: compressive strength minimum 2,500 psi at 28 days
- Grout strength: 2,000 psi minimum compressive strength
- Reinforcement: epoxy-coated bars in exterior walls

**Construction Quality:**
- Prism testing frequency: 1 set per 5,000 square feet of wall area
- Mortar joint thickness: 3/8 inch ± 1/8 inch
- Head joint filling: minimum 90% solid fill requirement
- Wall alignment: 1/4 inch maximum deviation in 10 feet

### 4. WATERPROOFING AND SEALANT TESTING

**Below-Grade Waterproofing:**
- Hydrostatic testing of foundation walls prior to backfilling
- Holiday testing of all membrane applications using high-voltage detector
- Seam testing of sheet membranes: minimum 2 pounds per linear inch peel strength
- Flood testing of plaza deck waterproofing systems

**Sealant Applications:**
- Adhesion testing on representative substrate samples
- Sealant installation during moderate weather: 40°F to 80°F ambient temperature
- Cure time verification before exposure to weather
- Annual inspection and maintenance program implementation

### 5. ROOFING SYSTEM QUALITY ASSURANCE

**Single-Ply Membrane Systems:**
- Seam testing using destructive and non-destructive methods
- Pull-out testing of mechanical fasteners: minimum 200 pounds withdrawal force
- Core cuts for insulation adhesion verification
- Electronic leak detection (ELD) testing post-installation

**Built-Up Roofing Systems:**
- Interply adhesion testing between membrane layers
- Bitumen temperature monitoring during application
- Aggregate embedment testing for ballasted systems
- Infrared thermography for moisture detection

### 6. MECHANICAL SYSTEM COMMISSIONING

**HVAC Performance Testing:**
- Air flow measurements at all terminal devices
- Refrigerant charge verification and leak testing
- Control sequence verification and calibration
- Energy efficiency testing per manufacturer specifications

**Plumbing System Testing:**
- Hydrostatic testing of all water supply piping at 150% working pressure
- Drainage system testing with water flow and smoke testing
- Backflow preventer testing and certification
- Hot water system temperature and flow rate verification

### 7. ELECTRICAL SYSTEM VERIFICATION

**Power Distribution Testing:**
- Insulation resistance testing of all feeders and branch circuits
- Ground fault testing and verification of protective device coordination
- Load testing of emergency generators under full rated load
- Arc flash analysis update with as-built electrical modifications

**Lighting and Controls:**
- Illumination level measurements per IES recommendations
- Daylight harvesting system calibration and commissioning
- Emergency lighting duration testing: minimum 90-minute operation
- Fire alarm system testing per NFPA 72 requirements

### 8. BUILDING ENVELOPE PERFORMANCE

**Air Leakage Testing:**
- Whole building pressurization testing per ASTM E779
- Individual unit testing in high-rise construction
- Target performance: 0.25 CFM per square foot at 75 Pa pressure differential
- Infrared thermography to identify thermal bridging and air leaks

**Water Penetration Testing:**
- Curtain wall mock-up testing per ASTM E1105
- Window and door installation testing
- Water spray testing of completed building sections
- Structural sealant testing for structural glazing systems

### 9. DOCUMENTATION AND REPORTING

**Daily Quality Reports:**
- Test results compilation and distribution within 24 hours
- Non-conformance documentation and corrective action plans
- Material delivery logs with acceptance/rejection decisions
- Weather conditions documentation affecting construction activities

**Final Quality Documentation:**
- Comprehensive test result compilation for building owner
- Warranty information and maintenance requirements
- As-built drawings reflecting field modifications
- Operations and maintenance manual preparation

### 10. THIRD-PARTY INSPECTION SERVICES

**Special Inspection Requirements:**
- Certified special inspectors for structural steel, concrete, and masonry
- Independent testing laboratory accreditation verification
- Inspector qualifications and certification maintenance
- Inspection scheduling coordination with construction activities

**Quality Assurance Program:**
- Regular audit of testing procedures and equipment calibration
- Proficiency testing participation for laboratory services
- Quality control manual updates and revision tracking
- Client reporting protocols and communication procedures
